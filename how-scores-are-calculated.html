Methodology

How scores are calculated

Every score Amplify produces is grounded in established research, published standards, and real conversion data. Here is what we measure, how we measure it, and the sources behind each signal.

Human Mode evaluates your site the way a potential client does, forming impressions in seconds, scanning for trust signals, and deciding whether you are worth reaching out to. The eight criteria below reflect the dimensions that research consistently shows influence whether a visitor becomes an enquiry.

01
Audience Resonance

Does this site feel made for the right client? Within seconds of landing, a potential client should feel like the site is speaking directly to them.

First impressions form in as little as 50 milliseconds
Users give a homepage 3 to 5 seconds to earn trust
75% of consumers judge credibility based on website design alone
Specific, targeted messaging converts 202% better than generic copy
Only 13% of customers feel brands truly understand their needs
Signals measured
  • AI
    5-second clarity test: can an AI reading the homepage answer what this agency does, who they serve, and why a client should care?
  • AI
    Audience specificity: does the copy call out a specific type of client, industry, or pain point rather than speaking to everyone?
  • AI
    Pain point language: does the homepage reflect the actual problems potential clients have, in the words they would use?
  • AI
    Generic claim detection: hollow buzzwords ("results-driven," "passionate," "full-service") with no supporting specifics lower the score.
  • AI
    Hero headline clarity: does the H1 communicate the value proposition immediately, or is it vague?
  • Programmatic
    Above-fold content presence: is there meaningful text above the fold, or only a full-screen image or video with no copy?
Sources
  • 50ms First Impression Study (Behaviour & Information Technology)
  • First Impressions (Nielsen Norman Group)
  • Five-Second Testing (Looppanel)
  • B2B Website Messaging Framework (Trajectory)
  • Personalized CTAs Convert 202% Better (HubSpot)
02
Content Quality

Is the writing compelling, clear, and professional? Errors and poor readability erode trust before a client has read a single sentence.

97.2% of people say grammar influences their perception of a company
Pages with spelling or grammar errors have 85% higher bounce rates
59% of consumers are less likely to trust a brand with copy errors
Ideal Flesch Reading Ease score for web content: 60 to 70 (8th to 9th grade level)
Signals measured
  • Programmatic
    Flesch Reading Ease score calculated from extracted page text. A score of 60 to 70 is the target. Below 50 is flagged.
  • Programmatic
    Sentence and paragraph length: a proxy for scannability. Long unbroken walls of text score lower.
  • Programmatic
    Content volume: is there enough copy for a reader to understand what this agency does, or is the page mostly images?
  • Programmatic
    Heading and subheading usage: does the page use H2s and H3s to break up content, or is it one uninterrupted block?
  • AI
    Grammar and spelling: AI reads the page copy and flags visible errors.
  • AI
    Passive voice density: excessive passive voice weakens authority and clarity.
  • AI
    Buzzword and jargon density: overlaps with Audience Resonance but scores how the copy reads, not who it speaks to.
  • AI
    Clarity per section: does each section say something meaningful, or is it filler?
Sources
  • Grammar Errors Lower Credibility (ResearchGate)
  • Typos, SEO and Conversions (Website Success)
  • Flesch Reading Ease Score (Yoast)
  • Flesch Kincaid Grade Level (Readable)
  • Website Readability (VWO)
03
Design & Experience

Does the site look credible and feel effortless to use? All signals in this criterion are grounded in the Laws of UX, a framework of established psychological and behavioral principles applied to interface design.

Signals measured via DOM analysis
  • Programmatic
    Hick's Law: decision time increases with the number of choices. Nav item count, visible CTAs, and form fields are checked. Menus exceeding seven items are flagged.
  • Programmatic
    Miller's Law: working memory holds approximately seven items. Elements competing for attention in key zones (nav, hero, footer) are counted and flagged when excessive.
  • Programmatic
    Fitts's Law: CTA button dimensions, touch target sizes, and spacing between clickable elements. Small or crowded targets score lower. This signal also feeds the Mobile Experience score.
  • Programmatic Context-dependent
    Jakob's Law: users expect sites to work like other sites. Standard pattern conformance is checked: logo placement, nav position, primary CTA, footer links.
  • Programmatic
    Serial Position Effect: the first and last items in a series are best remembered. Key content placement in nav, hero, and page structure is evaluated.
⚠️ Jakob's Law is context-dependent. Agencies with intentionally non-standard designs should not be penalized the same as those with oversight-driven deviations. Use this signal as a directional indicator, not a verdict.
Signals measured via AI visual analysis
  • AI
    Aesthetic-Usability Effect: beautiful design is perceived as more usable. Overall visual polish scored from a screenshot.
  • AI
    Von Restorff Effect: the element that stands out gets remembered. Does the primary CTA visually differentiate from everything around it?
  • AI Context-dependent
    Peak-End Rule: people judge an experience by its most intense moment and its ending. The hero (peak) and final CTA or footer (end) are evaluated.
  • AI
    Gestalt Laws (Proximity, Common Region, Similarity, Uniform Connectedness, Prägnanz): are related elements grouped? Is the layout clean or cluttered? Scored as one composite signal.
⚠️ Peak-End Rule is context-dependent. This signal assumes the hero is the site's strongest moment, which is not always the case. Use the score directionally and consider what your actual peak moment is.
Sources
  • Laws of UX (lawsofux.com)
04
Trust Signals

Does this site give a potential client enough evidence to feel safe handing over a project? All signals are evaluated on the homepage only. A link to a dedicated page counts as sufficient evidence.

72% of consumers find testimonials more credible than brand claims
Testimonials drive a 34% increase in conversions on sales pages
97% of consumers say reviews impact their purchasing decisions
5 or more reviews makes a service 270% more likely to convert
Above-fold award placement outperforms footer placement by 40 to 60% in A/B tests
Signals measured
  • Programmatic
    Testimonials: present on or linked from the homepage. Attributed testimonials (name, company, photo) score higher than anonymous ones.
  • Programmatic
    Case studies: link or reference to case study content. Quality signal: presence of quantified outcomes (problem, solution, result with numbers).
  • Programmatic
    Client logos: any client logos present on the homepage.
  • Programmatic
    Results and metrics: any quantified outcomes mentioned on the homepage ("increased conversions by 40%").
  • Programmatic
    Social proof volume: existence of reviews or ratings. Five or more is a meaningful threshold per research.
  • Programmatic
    Awards and recognition: any awards present. Placement is also scored: above-fold visibility is rated higher than footer placement.
  • Programmatic
    Team or About page: a homepage link or blurb counts as sufficient. Real people with names and faces signal legitimacy.
  • Programmatic
    Years in business or client count: any mention on the homepage counts.

05
Mobile Experience

A first impression increasingly happens on a phone. Does the site hold up when a potential client pulls it up on their device?

Mobile accounts for 62 to 64% of global web traffic as of 2026
Google minimum touch target: 48x48px with 8px spacing
Minimum body font size: 16px. Below this, iOS Safari auto-zooms.
Signals measured via DOM analysis
  • Programmatic
    Viewport meta tag: width=device-width, initial-scale=1 must be present. Without it, the page renders at desktop width and scales down.
  • Programmatic
    Font size: body text must meet the 16px minimum. Below this threshold, iOS auto-zooms on form fields.
  • Programmatic
    Touch targets: minimum 48x48px with 8px spacing per Google guidelines. This signal also feeds the Design and Experience score via Fitts's Law.
  • Programmatic
    Horizontal overflow: any content that bleeds beyond the viewport width and causes horizontal scrolling is flagged.
  • Programmatic
    Responsive breakpoints: does the CSS include mobile media queries at all?
Signals measured via AI visual analysis
  • AI
    Navigation usability: does the desktop nav collapse into something usable on mobile (hamburger menu, drawer, etc.)?
  • AI
    Content legibility: does the layout hold up at mobile viewport, or do elements overlap and stack awkwardly?
  • AI
    Intrusive interstitials: pop-ups or overlays that cover content on mobile. This signal also feeds the SEO score, as Google penalizes intrusive interstitials.
Sources
  • Mobile Market Share (StatCounter)
  • Mobile Traffic Percentage (MobiLoud)
  • Touch Target Size, Google Guidelines
  • Mobile Font Size Standards (FontFYI)
  • Size fonts and tap targets (Digital.gov)
06
SEO

Can a potential client find this agency when they search? All SEO signals are sourced directly from Google Search Central documentation.

Signals measured via DOM analysis
  • Programmatic
    Title tag: present, unique, under 60 characters, and keyword-relevant.
  • Programmatic
    Meta description: present and under 160 characters.
  • Programmatic
    H1 and heading hierarchy: one H1 present, logical H1 to H2 to H3 structure. Also feeds the Accessibility score.
  • Programmatic
    Image alt text: meaningful images have descriptive alt attributes. Also feeds the Accessibility score.
  • Programmatic
    Canonical tag: self-referencing canonical present.
  • Programmatic
    robots.txt and noindex: the page is not accidentally blocking crawlers.
  • Programmatic
    Structured data: any schema markup present on the homepage. This signal also feeds the AI Mode score.
  • Programmatic
    Intrusive interstitials: Google penalizes these on mobile. This signal also feeds the Mobile Experience score.
Signals measured via AI content analysis
  • AI
    Keyword relevance: does the page content reflect what the agency actually does, in language clients would search for?
  • AI
    Thin content: is there enough substance for Google to understand what this page is about?

07
Accessibility

Does the site work for everyone, regardless of how they experience the web? Amplify measures against the WCAG AA conformance level, the widely accepted standard referenced by courts and regulators globally.

Signals measured via DOM analysis
  • Programmatic
    Image alt text WCAG 1.1.1: all meaningful images have descriptive alt attributes. Also feeds SEO.
  • Programmatic
    Color contrast ratio WCAG 1.4.3: minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text.
  • Programmatic
    Form labels WCAG 1.3.1: all input fields have associated labels.
  • Programmatic
    Language attribute WCAG 3.1.1: <html lang=""> present so screen readers identify the language.
  • Programmatic
    Skip navigation link WCAG 2.4.1: allows keyboard users to skip past repeated nav.
  • Programmatic
    No auto-playing media WCAG 1.4.2: audio or video that plays without user action.
  • Programmatic
    Heading structure WCAG 1.3.1: logical, non-skipping hierarchy. Also feeds SEO.
Signals measured via AI visual analysis
  • AI
    Focus indicators WCAG 2.4.7: are interactive elements visually distinguishable when focused?
  • Phase 2
    Keyboard navigability: full keyboard-only navigation testing requires interaction and will be added in a future release.
Sources
  • WCAG, Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (W3C)
08
Contact & Conversion

A potential client has made it through the site and is ready to reach out. Does the site make that easy, or does it create friction at the worst possible moment?

Reducing a form from 11 to 4 fields increases conversions by 120%
Action-oriented CTAs convert 121% more than passive ones ("Learn More," "Submit")
First-person CTA phrasing boosts click-through by 90%
Above-fold CTAs outperform below-fold by 304%
Personalized CTAs convert 202% better than generic defaults
Signals measured via DOM analysis
  • Programmatic
    Contact information present: email address, phone number, or contact form visible on or linked from the homepage.
  • Programmatic
    CTA above the fold: at least one clear call-to-action is visible without scrolling.
  • Programmatic
    CTA repetition: does a CTA also appear near the bottom of the page? Ties back to the Peak-End Rule in Design and Experience.
  • Programmatic Context-dependent
    Form field count: fewer fields generally reduces friction. The research threshold is 4 or fewer for maximum conversion, but project inquiry forms reasonably require more context.
⚠️ Form field count is context-dependent. Fewer fields is not always better. A client requesting a project estimate expects to provide more detail than a newsletter subscriber. Use this signal directionally.
Signals measured via AI analysis
  • AI
    CTA clarity: is it obvious what happens when you click? Vague CTAs ("Learn more") score lower than action-oriented ones ("Start your project").
  • AI
    Response time promise: does the site mention how quickly they respond? Any commitment ("we'll get back to you within 24 hours") reduces friction.
  • AI
    Friction language near CTAs: does anything adjacent to the CTA create hesitation? Requiring account creation before contact, or no privacy reassurance near a form, will lower the score.

Important notice

Amplify scores are directional indicators grounded in established research and the collective expertise of Automattic. They are not definitive verdicts. A score reflects a point-in-time audit of your homepage and may not account for all site-specific context, audience nuances, or intentional design decisions.

Modifying your website based on Amplify recommendations is done at your own discretion and risk. Amplify does not guarantee specific outcomes as a result of acting on its recommendations. This includes changes to traffic, conversion rates, search rankings, or client enquiries.

Some signals in this tool are generated through AI analysis. These scores carry a higher degree of interpretive variance than programmatic checks and should be treated accordingly.
